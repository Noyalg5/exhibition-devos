Process Mapper

How the production model changes

① Phase 03 → 04  ·  Interpretation to Design

Content is locked late in the process, forcing expensive design rework and compressing the build schedule downstream.

② Phase 05  ·  Fabrication

Physical structures are built from scratch for each venue. No modularity means high cost, long lead times, and material waste.

③ Phase 08  ·  Evaluate & Strike

Evaluation only happens after the show closes — too late to improve the visitor experience or feed learning back into the tour.

The traditional model is strictly sequential and single-use: each phase waits for the previous one to complete, knowledge is siloed, and most physical build is discarded after each venue.

Participatory & community input

Community voices and local knowledge enter the process early — at the curatorial stage — shaping interpretation rather than being an add-on.

Reusable / modular build

Physical structures are designed from the outset as adaptable, re-skinnable modules. The same core build travels across all three venues.

Digital collaboration across cities

London, Dubai, and Riyadh teams share a live cloud workspace, enabling simultaneous local adaptation without losing coherence.

In the media-centric model, content and media sit at the core and are developed in parallel with all other phases. Physical elements are modular and reused across venues, community input feeds in early, and the three city teams collaborate digitally in real time — with structured feedback flowing back into the system after each opening.

Reusable / modular: area × reuse% × £280/m² paid once (initial build), then area × reuse% × £120/m² × venues (re-skin per venue).

Freight: logistics input × venues.

Design & interpretation: charged once across the whole programme.

Contingency: percentage applied on the sum of all above.

Revenue: £1,600,000 per venue (admission, venue license & touring premium) — stated and fixed for comparison purposes.

Margin: (revenue − cost) ÷ revenue.

Production time: a base lead-time (shortened by digital parallel working) + blended weeks per venue (10 wk new / 5 wk reuse) + 2-week freight buffer between venues.
